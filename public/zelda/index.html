The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time is an action-adventure game developed and published by Nintendo for the Nintendo 64 (N64) video game console. It was released in Japan in November 1998, and in North America and Europe in November 1999. Ocarina of Time is the fifth game in The Legend of Zelda series, and is the first to feature 3D graphics.

In Ocarina of Time, the player controls Link, a young Hylian who must travel through time to prevent Ganondorf, the King of Gerudo, from obtaining the Triforce, a sacred relic that grants wishes. Link travels through time as a child and as an adult, and must use his skills to solve puzzles, defeat enemies, and collect items to help him on his quest.

Ocarina of Time was a critical and commercial success, and is considered to be one of the greatest video games of all time. It was praised for its innovative graphics, gameplay, and story. Ocarina of Time sold over 7.6 million copies worldwide, and is the best-selling N64 game.
